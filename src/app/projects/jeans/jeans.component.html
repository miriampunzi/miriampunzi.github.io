Shopping for jeans and other form-fitting apparel online could be time-consuming and frustrating. It's hard to compare between different styles, and it's hard to know for sure how they will fit. It's a disappointing experience when you have to return an item that doesn't fit quite right. How can you help customers select the style of jeans that they’re looking for in the right size?

From: uxchallenge.co
Conducted open-ended interviews with 6 users (3 men, 3 women) to understand their online jeans shopping behavior:
  • Most avoid buying jeans online due to fear of poor fit.
  • Only 1 user had a successful online jeans purchase, described as “lucky.”
  • All participants prefer trying jeans in-store for assurance on fit and look.
  • Users find sizing inconsistent across brands, making size charts unreliable.
  • Even when following size guides, jeans often arrive ill-fitting (tight thighs, wrong length, etc.).
  • Visual appearance is key—users want to know how jeans will look on their body type.
  • Some showed interest in using an avatar to preview fit, but were unsure how accurate it would be.
: users need a more visual, personalized, and trustworthy way to evaluate fit and style online.
Application that helps users discover and select jeans that fit their unique body shape, while also providing expert advice and trend insights. The app aggregates jeans from multiple online providers, offering a personalized, educational, and confidence-boosting shopping experience.
  • A discovery hub where users can explore new jeans, follow current trends, and read expert advice from image consultants.
  • Educational content explains body shapes, jeans types, and how center of gravity affects fit.
  • Users can also see real-life photos from others with similar body types via a "See it on someone like me" feature.
  • Only providers who share exact garment measurements are included, ensuring a more accurate fit and reducing guesswork.
  • Users can set up their profile by entering detailed body measurements or uploading a photo—used to generate a personalized avatar through a learning model. This avatar helps visualize how jeans will fit, highlighting areas that may feel tight or loose.
  • The app also calculates body shape and center of gravity, offering tailored fit advice (e.g., users with a low center of gravity may look best in high-waisted styles).
  • As part of a confidence-boosting experience, users are shown celebrities with similar body shapes and fun statistics about their body type.
 A targeted search experience that allows users to filter jeans by type (e.g., skinny, cropped, high-waist), brand, or body shape. This helps narrow down options quickly and find jeans that are more likely to fit well and look great. A personal space where users can save their favorite jeans to a wishlist or cart. When ready, they’re directed to the provider’s platform to complete the purchase.
  • Chosen visual style: Pop Art, inspired by bold colors and graphic contrasts.
  • Historical reference: connects to the 1960s, when jeans became a symbol of individuality and rebellion.
  • Reflects the personality of jeans: expressive, bold, and versatile.
  • A creative challenge: stepping away from typical fashion app aesthetics to explore a more distinctive visual language.
